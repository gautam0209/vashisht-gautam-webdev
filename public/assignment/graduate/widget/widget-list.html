Jury To Decide Whether Value Actually Owns Dota 2

Two mobile developers, Lilith Games and uCool, have tried to make clones of Dota 2 for smartphones and been sued by Valve as a result. Their games, Dota Legends and Heroes Charge respectively, are slimmed down MOBAs that are popular in China. As Ars Technica reported, in response to Valve’s copyright claim against them, uCool has argued that the company doesn’t actually own the source material that Dota is based on, make its claim illegitimate.

Robots at TechFest at IIT Bombay

Since its inception in 1998, Techfest has stayed true to its motto of spreading the best of science and technology among the masses. It has slowly evolved into a universal platform to reach out to all tech-enthusiasts who wish to prove their mettle at the vanguard of technical competitions. Spreading its wings to the international arena, Techfest brings forth the International Robotics Challenge, a combat of robotic gladiators testing the very survival of the fittest.
